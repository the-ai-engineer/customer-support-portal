# Remote Working Policy

## Purpose

This policy explains how employees can work remotely while maintaining security, collaboration, and service quality.

## Standard Remote Working

Employees may work remotely up to two days per week if their role allows it and their manager approves the arrangement.

Remote working days should be agreed with the line manager and recorded in the team calendar.

Employees must remain available during their normal working hours unless they have agreed a different schedule.

## Working From Another Location

Employees who want to work from a different city or country must request approval from their manager and HR before making arrangements.

Working from another country may create legal, tax, payroll, security, or insurance issues.

Employees must not work from another country without written approval from HR.

## Equipment and Security

Employees must use company-approved devices and follow information security requirements when working remotely.

Confidential work should not be discussed where it can be overheard.

Company documents and devices must be kept secure at all times.

## Office Attendance

Employees may be asked to attend the office for team meetings, customer meetings, training, or business-critical activities.

## Questions

Questions about remote working should be sent to hr@example.com.
